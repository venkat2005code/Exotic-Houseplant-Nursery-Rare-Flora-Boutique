The Science of Variegation

Variegation is typically caused by a genetic mutation that prevents the production of chlorophyll in certain plant cells. Because these white or yellow sections cannot photosynthesize, highly variegated plants require significantly more light to sustain themselves and generally grow slower than their solid-green counterparts.
